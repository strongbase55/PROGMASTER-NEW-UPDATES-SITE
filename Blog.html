The ATS-Proof Resume

Recruiters spend 6 seconds scanning your resume. Here is how to make them count.

The Google XYZ Formula

Don't just list duties. Use this formula: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]."

Bad: "Wrote code for the login page."
Good: "Reduced login latency by 20% (Y) by refactoring the OAuth authentication flow (Z) for 50k+ daily users (X)."

Key Resume Sections

  • Header: Name, GitHub Link, Portfolio Link, LinkedIn (No full address).
  • Skills: Group them (e.g., Languages: JS, Python; Tools: Docker, AWS).
  • Experience: Reverse chronological. Focus on impact, not just tasks.
  • Projects: Critical for juniors. Show you can build.

Interview Preparation

1. The Behavioral Round

Prepare stories for the "Tell me about a time..." questions using the STAR method.

  • Situation: Set the scene.
  • Task: What was the goal?
  • Action: What specific steps did YOU take?
  • Result: What was the outcome? (Use numbers).

2. System Design (Mid/Senior)

Don't jump to coding. Ask clarifying questions first.

"Before we design the database schema for this URL shortener, I'd like to understand the constraints.
- What is the expected Read/Write ratio?
- Do we need immediate consistency or is eventual consistency okay?"

Salary Negotiation

You leave money on the table if you don't negotiate. It is expected in the tech industry.

Rule #1: Never give the first number

Recruiter: "What are your salary expectations?"
You: "Right now, I'm focused on finding the right fit for my skills. Could you share the budget range allocated for this position?"

The Counter Offer

If they offer $100k, but you want $115k:

"I'm really excited about the team and the mission. However, looking at the market rate for this role and my experience with [Specific Skill], I was expecting something closer to the $115k range. Is there flexibility to get closer to that number?"

The ATS-Proof Resume Guide

Your resume is not read by humans first; it's read by an Application Tracking System (ATS). Here is how to beat the bot.

1. Use Standard Formatting

Avoid double columns, images, or skill bars. Use standard headings like "Experience", "Education", and "Skills".

2. The STAR Method

Describe your experience using Situation, Task, Action, Result.

  • Bad: "Worked on API."
  • Good: "Designed a REST API (Action) for a high-traffic e-commerce platform (Situation) which reduced load times by 40% (Result)."
